Meenut-Intellectual Idolatry

What is the real source of the conflict between Chabad replacement theology and the theology of the non-Chassidic Torah sages? The real conflict is probably over ikkarim, i.e. fundamental principles of Judaism that Chabad is attempting to replace with Chabad's principles. For example, the Rambam's third ikkar that states that Hashem is not a body or a force in a body, and that a Jew who believes that Hashem has a body is a "min" who has no share in the world to come1.

The Rambam wrote that a Torah observant Jew must affirm the famous 13 ikkarim (principles) specified by the Rambam, otherwise that Jew is a transgressor and has excluded himself from the Jewish nation. In Mishneh Torah, Hilchot Teshuvah, Chapter Three, the Rambam lists various categories of sinners "that have no share in the World to Come but suffer excision and loss of identity, and are damned for ever and ever for their exceeding wickedness and sinfulness".

One such category of sinner who has no share in the world to come is a min. A מִין (min) is a halachic term that refers to a person who maintains an atheist or idolatrous intellectual concept of God, even if that person doesn't actually worship idols. In Hilchot Teshuvah 3:7, the Rambam lists five types of minim:

"There are five categories of minim (1) he who says that there is no God and no Omnipotence; (2) he who says that there is an Omnipotence but that there are two or more such; (3) he who says that there is One Lord; but that He is corporeal and has a form; (4) Likewise one who says that He alone is not the First Cause and Creator of all; (5) likewise he who worships a star, or planet, or any other as a mediator between him and the Lord of the universe; every one of these five is a min.”

In a video linked here, a certain "rabbi" I refer to as an "Apostle of Chabad" claims that:

"...the Rebbe is beyond our comprehension, the Rebbe is Ein Sof...the Rebbe is Elokus..." Should a Lubavitcher chassid read biographies written about the Rebbe?

Ein Sof is a term used by Kabbalists to refer to Hashem. Elokus is a term that refers to the properties of Hashem. When certain radical Chabad rabbis claim that "the Rebbe is beyond our comprehension, the Rebbe is Ein Sof...the Rebbe is Elokus...", this creates a conflict over the fundamentals of Torah theology that has nothing to do with chakira (i.e. philosophy). But such absurd claims such as “the Rebbe is Elokus” by the radical Chabad rabbis essentially eliminate any real daylight between ancient Jewish-Christian sects and present day Chabad. It is then absurd to label this dispute as a dispute over the legitimacy of "philosophy".

In my opinion these claims by "Apostles of Chabad" constitute "meenut", i.e. a form of intellectual idolatry. A new religion resembling early Christianity is being promoted here that is utterly in conflict with authentic Judaism. Much as ancient renegade idolatrous Jews promoted early Christianity.

I do not claim that all Chabadniks accept the radical Christianized viewpoints in that video. However, the fact that various persons promoting such severe distortions of Torah principles seem to be tolerated, and they are not completely ejected from and ostracized from the Chabad movement, is rather disturbing.

It is certainly possible that only a minority of Chabadniks accept the radical Christianized claims made by the "Apostles of Chabad". But the numbers and influence of such persons may not be insignificant, and these people may at times conceal their beliefs.
